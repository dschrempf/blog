Research interests

I am excited about studying the fundamentals of nature. My general research interests can broadly be divided into a passion towards mathematical modeling and parameter inference from a variety of closely related species.

On the one hand, I am interested in the theoretical basis and analytical solutions of multivariate models in population genetics and especially in the foundation of discrete approaches such as the multivariate Wright-Fisher as well as the Moran model. A thorough understanding of these models requires analysis of the respective discrete-state Markov chains in discrete and continuous time, examination of their long term behavior, equilibria and their relationship to each other. I am particularly intrigued by the diffusion approximations of these models. I would like to answer questions like, what the connections between continuous and discrete approaches are or how they relate to numerical approximations or moment-based estimations available in the literature.

I employ my findings in phylogenetics to account for population genetic effects such as shared ancestral polymorphisms and incomplete lineage sorting. We have termed our approach Polymorphism-aware phylogenetic Model (PoMo). I use computer simulations to show that PoMo is fast and accurate as well as robust when model assumptions are violated. By analyzing closely related species, I hope to discover new estimates of divergence times, germline mutation rates or tree topologies and to contribute to the understanding of the molecular basis of speciation.
