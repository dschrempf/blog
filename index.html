7 Research interests

I am excited about nature and how the determining processes work and play together. By developing mathematical models and by exploration and examination of current data I hope to improve our understanding of these processes.

During my PhD I focused on the theoretical basis and analytical solutions of multivariate models in population genetics and especially in the foundation of discrete approaches such as the multivariate Wright-Fisher as well as the Moran model. A thorough understanding of these models requires analysis of the respective discrete-state Markov chains in discrete and continuous time, examination of their long term behavior, equilibria and their relationship to each other. I became particularly intrigued by the diffusion approximations of these models. I worked on questions like, what the connections between continuous and discrete approaches are or how they relate to numerical approximations or moment-based estimations available in the literature.

I employed my findings in phylogenetics to account for population genetic effects such as shared ancestral polymorphisms and incomplete lineage sorting (Polymorphism-aware phylogenetic Model, PoMo). I used computer simulations to show that our approach is fast and accurate as well as robust when model assumptions are violated. Our analyses of closely related species revealed new estimates of divergence times, germline mutation rates and tree topologies — a contribution towards understanding the molecular basis of speciation.

Have a look at my publications on ORCID.
